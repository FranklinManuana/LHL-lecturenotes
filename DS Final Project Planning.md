## 1. Project Planning

### a) Problem Statement

Define the problem your project addresses. Include:

- The problem to be solved
- Why solving this problem is valuable

### b) Data Sources

Identify relevant datasets for your project. Include:

- Data source location
- Overview of the data
- Potential challenges in using the data

### c) Approach Outline

Describe your planned approach for solving the problem. Include:

- Key steps in your analysis
- Algorithms or techniques you plan to use

---

## 2. Project Setup

### a) Environment Setup

Set up your development environment:

- Install necessary libraries
- Configure your workspace (e.g., Jupyter Notebook or IDE)

### b) Data Preparation

Prepare your data for analysis:

- Load datasets
- Clean and preprocess data

---

## 3. Project Workflow

### a) Research and Analysis

Conduct your research and perform exploratory data analysis (EDA):

- Identify patterns and insights
- Visualize key findings

### b) Model Development

Develop your machine learning models:

- Train and validate models
- Evaluate performance metrics

---

## 4. Project Development

Refine your work according to project milestones:

- Implement iterative improvements based on feedback
- Document your methodology

---

## 5. Project Deployment

Prepare your project for delivery:

- Finalize data visualizations
- Prepare scripts or notebooks for reproducibility
- Optionally deploy your model using a platform like Flask, FastAPI, or Streamlit

---

## 6. Project Presentation

### Final Project Presentation

- Students will prepare a five-minute presentation for their final lecture about their project.
- a Dry Run of this presentation must be done by Tuesday of your final week
- students are invited to sign up to a quarterly Demo Day to present their final project to a bigger audience
- students will submit a video of their final presentation that can be showcased to LHL's network of employers and social media followers

**Deadlines:**

- Practice Presentation (with mentors): Tuesday of Final Week
- Presentation: During a final lecture.
